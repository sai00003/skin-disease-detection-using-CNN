Skin Disease Information

Skin diseases can vary greatly in appearance and severity. Some common types include:

  • Melanocytic Nevi: Common moles that are usually harmless.
  • Melanoma: A serious form of skin cancer that can spread to other parts of the body.
  • Benign Keratosis-like Lesions: Non-cancerous growths that look like warts or patches.
  • Basal Cell Carcinoma: The most common type of skin cancer, usually caused by sun exposure.
  • Actinic Keratoses: Rough, scaly patches that can develop into skin cancer.
  • Vascular Lesions: Abnormal blood vessel growths, including spider veins and port-wine stains.
  • Dermatofibroma: Benign tumors of the skin that are usually firm and elevated.
